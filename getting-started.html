Getting Started with Genealogy

Starting your family history journey can feel overwhelming, but genealogy becomes easier when you begin with the information closest to you. Start by gathering names, dates, and places from family members and family records.

Step 1: Start with Yourself

Write down your full name, birth date, and birthplace. Then add information about your parents, grandparents, and great-grandparents if available.

Step 2: Talk to Family Members

Interview parents, grandparents, aunts, uncles, and other relatives. Ask about names, marriages, military service, family traditions, and places where family members lived.

Step 3: Collect Important Records

Gather birth certificates, marriage records, death certificates, family Bibles, photo albums, obituaries, and letters. These documents help confirm family connections.

Step 4: Organize Your Findings

Use a notebook, folders, or genealogy software to track the people you research. Staying organized will help you avoid repeating work later.
