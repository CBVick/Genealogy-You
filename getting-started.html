Getting Started with Genealogy

Beginning your family history journey can feel overwhelming at first, but it becomes easier when you start with the information closest to you and work backward one generation at a time.

Start with Yourself

Record your full name, birth date, and birthplace. Then add information for your parents, grandparents, and great-grandparents if known.

Talk to Family

Interview relatives and ask about family names, marriages, military service, traditions, and the places where family members lived.

Gather Records

Collect birth certificates, marriage records, obituaries, family Bibles, photographs, letters, and other documents that help confirm relationships.

Stay Organized

Use folders, notebooks, or genealogy software to keep track of your research and avoid repeating work later.

    Focus on accuracy instead of speed. Genealogy research is strongest when each generation is carefully verified.
