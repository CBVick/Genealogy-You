Research Resources

Genealogy research combines online tools, historical documents, and local records. Use the resources below to explore your family history and verify your findings.

Online Databases

Search large collections of historical records.

  • Familysearch.org – Historical archives and family records

Government Records

Official documents provide reliable evidence of family history.

  • National Archives (archives.gov) – Federal records, census data, and military documents
  • Fold3.com – Military records and historical documents

Cemeteries & Obituaries

Find burial locations, memorials, and family connections.

  • Find a Grave – Cemetery records and memorial pages
  • Newspapers.com – Historical newspapers and obituaries
  • GenealogyBank – Newspaper archives and family records

Libraries & Local Records

Visit local libraries, courthouses, and historical societies for records not available online.

  • Public libraries
  • County courthouses
  • Historical societies

Family Documents

Personal records often contain details not found elsewhere.

  • Family Bibles
  • Old photographs
  • Letters and journals
  • Certificates

Research Tip

Always compare information from multiple sources. Names, dates, and spellings may vary, so verifying across records improves accuracy.
