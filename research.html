Research Resources

Genealogy research uses both online and local sources. The best results usually come from comparing several records instead of relying on only one source.

Online Databases

Popular websites include Ancestry, FamilySearch, Find a Grave, and census record collections. These tools can help you locate names, dates, and family connections. https://www.findagrave.com/

Libraries and Archives

Public libraries, local archives, and historical societies often have newspapers, city directories, family histories, and records not available online. https://www.genealogybank.com/ https://www.newspapers.com/

Government Records

Birth, marriage, death, land, probate, and military records can provide evidence of family relationships and important life events.

Cemeteries and Obituaries

Cemetery records, tombstones, and obituaries often include dates, relatives, and places that can lead to additional discoveries.

Family Documents

Old photographs, letters, family Bibles, certificates, and oral history from relatives can provide valuable clues and personal details.

Research Tip

Always compare facts from multiple sources. Spellings, ages, and dates may vary, so cross-checking helps improve accuracy.
